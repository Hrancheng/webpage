My Projects

Android app with RNN for voice identification

This is an Android app we developed with RNN model, able to identify Quran verses with the users' recitation. We use Python package to transform voice information to Numpy array. And trained the model with peak loss smaller than 0.1. We collected ground-truth data from Youtube recitation videos. We are still working to improve the accuracy of the model and publish the app to Google Play Store.

Super resolution technique implementation: SRCNN, SRGAN and Bicubic

Submitted as the final project for undergraduate-level computer vision course, we built wheel to compare complexity and efficiency of three classical super resolution: SRCNNN, SRGAN and Bicubic. we run and reimplement one traditional technique Bicubic Interpolation and two deep learning methods SRCNN and SRGAN over one common dataset DIV2K dataset to compare them in terms of accuracy and efficiency.

Team strength estimation based on Bradley-Terry model with data from NCAA women's basketball

AWe test 6 different models to see which one does the best job of predicting who will win the NCAA tournament. Those models differ in whether to incorporate home advantage variable and date interaction term, as well as whether there is penalty term added to the model formula. and then from there we use cross-validation to compute negative log likelihood loss, hoping to see which model to prefer and then ranking the teams based on the model.

Bayesian analysis of factors causing heart disease

We use Bayesian model to analyze the factors causing heart disease. Also, we use Markov Chain Monte Carlo simulation to assess the performance of our proposed models. To effectively facilitate our computational process, we randomly sample 4000 observations as our dataset of interest.
